# Hormozi Skill: Offer Construction

Use when: you need to design or improve an offer so it converts at higher prices without competing on commodity pricing.

## Core principles
- A **Grand Slam Offer** makes your product hard to compare and easier to buy.
- Value is perceived through the **Value Equation**, not your effort.
- Optimize all four value levers: increase Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood; decrease Time Delay + Effort/Sacrifice.
- Improve price tolerance with offer design first (proof, guarantees, delivery), not discounts first.
- Use enhancement levers (scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees, naming) ethically and specifically.

## Key frameworks
### 1) The Value Equation
**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Diagnostic prompts:
- Dream Outcome: What exact end state do they want?
- Likelihood: Why should they believe this will work for *them*?
- Time Delay: How fast can they see first meaningful win?
- Effort/Sacrifice: How much work, uncertainty, or lifestyle cost is required?

### 2) Grand Slam Offer Construction (Problems → Solutions → Trim & Stack)
From the source flow:
1. List painful problems and desired outcomes by avatar.
2. Map each to a concrete solution component.
3. **Trim & Stack**: remove weak/unclear elements; stack clear, high-value deliverables.
4. Sequence deliverables so objection-handling is built in (before/at purchase/after purchase).
5. Add enhancement levers (below).

### 3) Offer Enhancement Levers
- **Scarcity**: limited quantity/capacity/access.
- **Urgency**: limited time/decision window.
- **Bonuses**: targeted add-ons that reduce delay/effort or increase likelihood.
- **Guarantees**: risk reversal tied to specific behavior and outcomes.
- **Naming**: clear, concrete, outcome-biased naming.

## Practical templates
### Template A: Value Equation Rewrite
- Dream Outcome we promise: `[specific outcome]`
- Why they believe it: `[proof + mechanism + who it worked for]`
- Time-to-first-win: `[X days/weeks to first milestone]`
- Effort required from buyer: `[minimum required actions]`
- Final offer line: `Get [outcome] in [time] with [low-effort mechanism], even if [common fear].`

### Template B: Trim & Stack Grid
- Problem: `[pain]`
- Core deliverable: `[main component]`
- Support deliverable: `[bonus/checklist/tool]`
- Objection neutralized: `[time/money/trust/fit]`
- Stage used: `[pre-sale | onboarding | delivery | retention]`

### Template C: Ethical Scarcity/Urgency Copy
- Scarcity: `We can onboard only [N] new clients this cycle due to [real constraint].`
- Urgency: `Enrollment closes [date/time] because [delivery timeline/capacity reason].`

## Examples
1. Agency reposition:
- Commodity: “Monthly ads management.”
- Grand Slam: “Pay one-time setup + ad spend; we launch, optimize, and hand off a lead engine in 45 days.”

2. Coaching offer:
- Increase likelihood with weekly reviews + dashboard.
- Reduce effort with done-for-you templates and SOPs.

3. Service offer guarantee:
- `If milestone X is not reached by day Y and you complete agreed actions, we continue at no additional fee until milestone is hit.`
