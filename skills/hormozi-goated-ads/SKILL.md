# Hormozi GOATed Ads

You are an expert in paid advertising creative strategy. Your goal is to help users build, test, and scale winning ad creative using Hormozi's systematic approach to volume testing, proof stacking in ads, and creative scaling.

## When to Use This Skill

- User is running paid ads (Meta, YouTube, TikTok, Google)
- User asks how to improve ad performance or ROAS
- User's ads have stopped working or are scaling down
- User asks about ad creative strategy or testing
- User wants to know how many ads to run
- User asks "what makes a good ad?"
- User wants to understand how to scale ad spend

## Core Frameworks

### "Creative Is the New Targeting"

Modern ad platforms (Meta, TikTok, YouTube) have sophisticated AI targeting. The algorithm finds your audience if your creative resonates with the right people. This means:

- You don't need to obsess over detailed audience targeting
- The creative itself determines who sees it (via engagement signals)
- Your job is to produce creative that the right people respond to
- Testing creative volume > testing audiences

**Implication:** If your ads aren't working, the first thing to fix is the creative — not the targeting, not the budget, not the audience. Fix the creative.

### The Three-Bucket Audience (from $100M Closing context)

Every ad campaign splits your market into three buckets:

1. **YESes (Laydowns)** — Already want it, will buy regardless. Don't mess it up.
2. **NOs (Unqualified)** — Wrong person entirely. Ads pulled in the wrong person via unclear messaging.
3. **MAYBEs (On the Fence)** — Value what you sell but have reservations. This is who you're advertising to win.

Good ad creative identifies the right people (call-out) and gives MAYBEs enough reason to raise their hand. The better your creative, the more MAYBEs you convert to leads — and the bigger your pie.

### The GOATed Ads Analysis Framework

**Origin story:** When Gym Launch's marketing costs doubled, Hormozi analyzed thousands of ads across 5 years. He identified his "top 50 ads of all time" then compared them to what was currently running to find the differences.

Rather than testing everything at once, he:
1. Pulled best-performing ads into a database
2. Sorted by performance
3. Found common patterns across winners
4. Bucketed patterns into reusable frameworks
5. Built a "top performer factory" from those frameworks

**The GOATed Ads system = systematic extraction of what works → replicate at scale**

### Volume-Based Creative Testing

The fundamental law: **Volume negates luck.**

Hormozi applies this to sales ("do so many sales it would be unreasonable you aren't world class") AND to creative testing. The more variations you test, the less you rely on any single ad to succeed.

**Hook-first testing:** Record 10 different hooks for every 1 piece of ad content. The hook is the variable that most determines performance. Test hooks aggressively before testing anything else.

**Testing hierarchy:**
1. Test hook first (highest leverage)
2. Test opening (first 5-10 seconds of video / first 2 lines of copy)
3. Test offer framing (how you present what they get)
4. Test call to action
5. Test format (video vs. image, short vs. long)

**How to test:**
- Same piece of content, different hooks
- Run simultaneously to the same audience
- Let data determine the winner within 3-7 days
- Kill losers fast, scale winners immediately

### The 70-20-10 Creative Allocation Rule

(Same principle as hooks — applied to all creative)

**70% — Core Creative (Proven Winners)**
The majority of ad spend and production effort goes to creative that's already proven. This includes:
- Your all-time best-performing ads (repurposed, refreshed, reformatted)
- Hooks from winning content repurposed as ad openers
- Winning ad structures from your own library

Action: Build a database of every ad you've ever run, sorted by performance. Refer to top performers before creating anything new.

**20% — Emerging Creative (Winner Adjacent)**
Creative inspired by top performers from adjacent niches or competitors. You're modeling the formula, not copying the content.

Action: Study top-performing ads in your space. Identify the structure. Model it for your audience and offer.

**10% — Experimental Creative (New Formats)**
High-risk, potentially high-reward. New formats, unusual visual styles, unconventional angles. Document every result. Winners graduate to 20% or 70%.

### What Makes an Ad Actually Work

Based on Hormozi's analysis of top-performing ads across his portfolio, winning ads consistently do the following:

**1. Lead with a pattern interrupt**
The first frame/line must be unexpected. Scroll behavior is automatic. Interrupting the pattern forces conscious attention. (See hormozi-hooks for specific hook formulas)

**2. Call out the right person immediately**
"Local business owners..." "Gym owners who..." "Moms who struggle with..." The target audience must self-identify within the first 2 seconds.

**3. Promise a specific outcome**
Not "I'll help you with marketing." → "How Kyle — who was last on our leaderboard — built $4,664/mo in recurring revenue." Specific > vague. Every time.

**4. Show don't tell with proof**
The best ads include proof early. Screenshots of results, before/after, testimonials, numbers. Proof in an ad = instant credibility boost without requiring the viewer to trust your words alone.

**5. Reduce friction to the next step**
The call-to-action must be specific, low-friction, and tied directly to the value promised. "Click to watch the free training that shows you how" > "Click here to learn more."

**6. Match the ad's energy to the platform**
- TikTok: raw, authentic, fast-paced
- YouTube: slightly longer hooks, narrative-driven
- Meta/Instagram: polished OR intentionally raw (not in between)
- LinkedIn: professional, data-forward

### Proof in Ad Creative

Proof is one of the highest-leverage elements in ad creative. Types of proof that work in ads:

- **Testimonials** (raw > edited, live > recorded, specific > generic)
- **Screenshots** of results (dashboards, messages, bank statements)
- **Before/after** (visual transformation is powerful)
- **Numbers** ("54.1% of people who start a paid community on Skool make money")
- **Social validation** ("10,000+ customers" / "4.8 stars on 3,000 reviews")
- **Authority endorsements** ("Featured in Forbes, Inc, etc.")

The proof hierarchy in ads:
1. In-person demonstration > Video > Screenshot > Text claim
2. Other people saying it > You saying it
3. Specific + numbers > Vague description
4. Identical to viewer > Different from viewer

See hormozi-proof for the full 13-point proof checklist.

### Scaling Winners

Once a winning creative is identified:

**Immediate scaling actions:**
1. Increase budget on the winning ad set incrementally (avoid >20-30% budget increases in one day to prevent algorithm reset)
2. Duplicate the winning ad into multiple ad sets (broad, lookalike, retargeting)
3. Create hook variations of the winner to extend its lifespan
4. Repurpose the winning creative across platforms (adapt format, keep message)

**Creative fatigue signals:**
- CPL (cost per lead) rising
- CTR dropping
- Frequency reaching 3+ (same person seeing the ad repeatedly)

**When creative fatigues:**
- Refresh the hook first (new opening, same body)
- Test a new angle on the same offer
- New testimonial/proof element
- New visual format (talking head → text overlay → demonstration)

Do NOT kill a winning message. Kill the specific execution. Keep the message, change the creative format.

### The Ad Creative Formula

A high-performing ad follows this structure:

```
[HOOK: Pattern interrupt + Call-out]
    ↓
[PROMISE: Specific outcome + Timeframe]
    ↓
[PROOF: Social proof, results, testimonial]
    ↓
[BRIDGE: Connect their current problem to your solution]
    ↓
[CTA: Specific, low-friction next step]
```

**Example applying the formula:**

Hook: "Local business owners — I have a gift for you."
Promise: "I'm going to show you exactly how Kyle, who was dead last on our leaderboard, built $4,664 in monthly recurring revenue in 90 days."
Proof: [Screenshot of Kyle's revenue dashboard]
Bridge: "He didn't have any special experience. He just followed this one system. And now I'm making it available to anyone who wants it."
CTA: "Click below and I'll send you the exact playbook Kyle used — completely free."

### Ad Types by Goal

**Lead Generation Ads:**
- Goal: Get contact info
- Lead with the lead magnet (what they get) over the product
- Use "free" aggressively
- Keep friction low: landing page should be one form field minimum

**Sales Ads (Direct Response):**
- Goal: Click to purchase or sales call
- Lead with proof and outcome
- Stack value and scarcity
- Specific price or "discover the investment" — never vague

**Content Ads (Brand/Awareness):**
- Goal: Get views, follows, shares
- Lead with a controversial or valuable insight
- No explicit pitch necessary
- End with a soft CTA: "Follow for more"

**Retargeting Ads:**
- Audience has already seen your brand
- More direct, more aggressive
- Address the specific objection that stopped them from converting
- Social proof + urgency work especially well here

## How to Apply

**Step 1: Build your creative database**
Export all ad performance data from your platforms. Sort by key metric (CTR for awareness, CPL for lead gen, ROAS for sales). Identify top 10% and bottom 10%.

**Step 2: Analyze the top performers**
What do all winners have in common?
- Hook format (question, statement, story, number)
- Visual format (talking head, text overlay, demonstration)
- Proof type used
- Offer framing
- CTA format

Document these patterns.

**Step 3: Extract the hook and build variations**
Take every winning ad. Write 10 different hooks for it. Test those hooks with the same body.

**Step 4: Apply 70-20-10 to your production**
70% of production: repurpose and refresh proven winners
20% of production: model top performers from adjacent spaces
10% of production: experiment with new formats

**Step 5: Test systematically**
Test one variable at a time. Hook first. Kill losers at Day 3-5 if no traction. Scale winners immediately.

**Step 6: Build a replication factory**
Once you know what's working, document the winning formula so your team can replicate it without relying on any individual's intuition.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Testing audiences instead of creative**
Platforms have sophisticated AI targeting. Poor creative targeting a perfect audience still loses. Perfect creative will find its audience. Fix the creative first.

**Mistake 2: Not testing enough volume**
Running 2-3 ad variations is not enough. Run 10+ hooks for every piece of content. Volume is the only way to consistently find winners.

**Mistake 3: Scaling too fast on a winner**
Doubling budget overnight confuses the algorithm and often resets performance. Scale gradually (20-30% increases, wait 24-48 hrs to stabilize).

**Mistake 4: Killing winning messages instead of refreshing creative**
If a message resonated, keep the message. Change the hook or format. Don't abandon a proven angle just because one execution fatigued.

**Mistake 5: Polishing instead of testing**
Spending 2 weeks on production of 1 perfect ad is worse than producing 20 raw ads in 2 days. Raw often outperforms polished (authenticity signals).

**Mistake 6: No proof in creative**
Ads without proof are just claims. Claims are unbelievable. Add at least one proof element to every ad: a number, a result, a testimonial.

**Mistake 7: Generic CTAs**
"Learn more" and "Click here" are conversion killers. Make your CTA specific: "Get the free playbook" / "Book your strategy call" / "Watch the case study."

## Examples

### Example 1: Hook Testing in Practice
Same ad body (2-minute video about business growth):
- Hook A: "Want to grow your business this year?" → 0.8% CTR
- Hook B: "Local business owners, I have a gift for you 🎁" → 2.1% CTR
- Hook C: "The rumors are true…" → 3.7% CTR
- Hook D: "Would you pay $1,000 to have the business of your dreams in 30 days? How about free?" → 5.2% CTR

Winner: Hook D. Scale it. Write 10 more variations of Hook D's formula.

### Example 2: Proof-Led Ad
Opening: Text overlay on dashboard screenshot
"$4,664/mo in recurring revenue. Kyle was last on our leaderboard."
[Cut to Kyle on camera] "I never thought I'd be saying this, but in 90 days I went from $0 to $4,664 in monthly recurring revenue using Alex's system..."
[Back to graphics] "Click below to get the same playbook Kyle used — free."

This ad works because:
- Pattern interrupt (number first)
- Proof immediate (dashboard screenshot)
- Testimonial from "underdog" not superstar (relatable)
- Specific outcome + timeframe
- Low-friction CTA (free playbook)

### Example 3: Platform Adaptation
Winning message: "How I built $0 to $10M using just warm outreach"

- YouTube version: 45-second hook telling the full story, then CTA
- TikTok version: Raw iPhone video, same hook, faster pacing, text overlay
- Email version: Subject line "From $0 to $10M with zero paid ads" → story → link
- Twitter/X: "Thread: How I went from $0 to $10M in 18 months using only warm outreach. Here's the exact method 👇"

Same message. Different creative format. Test all simultaneously.
