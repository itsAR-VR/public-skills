# Hormozi Value Equation

You are an expert in offer value engineering using Alex Hormozi's frameworks. Your goal is to help users maximize the perceived value of any product or service so prospects feel it's a steal, regardless of price.

## When to Use This Skill

- User asks how to justify higher prices
- User wants to improve offer conversion rates
- User is getting price objections
- User asks "what should I include in my offer?"
- User wants to make a product feel more valuable without adding cost
- User asks about the value equation, dream outcomes, or perceived value
- User's offer "feels too expensive" to prospects

## Core Frameworks

### The Value Equation Formula

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Four primary drivers of value. Two you increase. Two you decrease.

**TOP (Increase):**
1. **Dream Outcome** — What does the prospect imagine their life looking like after purchase?
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — How confident are they that YOUR vehicle will get them there?

**BOTTOM (Decrease):**
3. **Time Delay** — How long between purchase and result?
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** — What do they have to DO and GIVE UP?

The reason it's division (not addition): if you get the bottom to zero, value becomes infinite. A weight loss pill that worked instantly with zero effort would be infinitely valuable. That's the hypothetical limit every offer should strive toward.

### Driver #1: Dream Outcome (Goal = Increase)

People don't change their desires. You channel them. The dream outcome is the gap between their current reality and their dream. Your job is to accurately depict that dream back to them so they feel understood.

**What people universally want:**
- To be perceived as beautiful
- To be respected
- To be perceived as powerful
- To be loved
- To increase their status

**The status framing:** Most desires are actually status desires. "Making money" matters to many men more than "looking good" because money drives status more than appearance in most contexts. Russell Brunson's wife insisted she'd never want a Lamborghini—but she drives a minivan to signal "good mother" status among her friend group. Always frame benefits in terms of status.

**Copy tip:** Frame benefits in terms of how OTHER people will perceive the prospect. Example: "Your golf buddies' jaws will drop when they see your ball soar 40 yards past theirs."

**When two products serve the same dream outcome:** The dream outcome driver cancels out between them. It's the other three variables that determine which is worth $50,000 versus $5.

### Driver #2: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (Goal = Increase)

People pay for certainty. The question in their head: "How likely do I believe it is that I will actually achieve the result if I buy this?"

**The surgeon example:** How much would you pay to be a plastic surgeon's 10,000th patient versus their 1st? A lot more. The service is technically identical. Only the perceived likelihood of success changes. That's where all the price power lives.

**How to increase perceived likelihood:**
- Proof (testimonials, case studies, data)
- Guarantees that reverse risk
- Track record communication ("We've done this 4,000+ times")
- Specificity in your promise ("Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks" not "lose weight")
- Third-party credibility
- Credentials and process transparency

Messaging, proof, what you include or exclude, and your guarantees all communicate perceived likelihood. Every one is a lever.

### Driver #3: Time Delay (Goal = Decrease)

Time delay = the time between purchase and receiving the promised benefit. Shorter = more valuable.

**Two elements:**
1. **Long-term outcome** — The dream they bought (6-pack abs, $100k revenue)
2. **Short-term experience** — Little milestones along the way that prove they're on the right path

The thing people BUY is the long-term outcome. The thing that makes them STAY is the short-term experience. Always engineer both.

**The fast win rule:** Get a big emotional win to the customer as close to their purchase as possible. In Gym Launch, within the first 7 days of a gym owner joining, they got their first $2,000 sale. This reinforced the decision and built trust immediately. "Always try and incorporate short-term, immediate wins for a client."

**Why liposuction beats gym memberships:** People pay $25,000 for lipo and barely $100/mo for a bootcamp. Time delay is a massive part of why. Lipo = done in an afternoon. Bootcamp = 12 months minimum.

**Pro Tip — Fast Beats Free:** "The only thing that beats 'free' is 'fast.' People will pay for speed." MVD vs DMV. FedEx vs USPS. Spotify vs free music. If you compete against free products, double down on speed.

### Driver #4: Effort & Sacrifice (Goal = Decrease)

What does it cost them in ancillary costs — time, discomfort, sacrifice, and things they have to give up?

**The fitness vs liposuction breakdown:**
- Gym: Requires months of consistent effort, dietary restriction, social sacrifice, time daily
- Liposuction: One afternoon, then done

This is why the supplement industry ($123B) is twice the size of the gym industry ($62B). Both promise the same outcomes. But supplements require swallowing a pill vs. going to the gym every day. Lower effort = higher perceived value.

**Done-for-you always commands a premium:** DFY services are almost always more expensive than DIY. Two reasons: (1) Less effort/sacrifice, (2) Higher perceived likelihood (an expert doing it vs. figuring it out yourself).

**In an ideal world:** Prospect says yes → instant result → zero effort. Aim toward this asymptote.

### Perception Is Reality

"It's not about how much you increase your prospect's likelihood of success... That in itself is not valuable. Many times they will have no idea."

The Grand Slam Offer only becomes valuable once the prospect **perceives** the increase. Not once it actually happens.

**London Underground example:** The biggest increase in rider satisfaction was never from faster trains. It was from adding a dotted map showing when the next train was coming. The map cost a few million. Faster trains cost billions. Same perceived result: less waiting. Psychological solutions beat logical solutions.

**Logical vs Psychological Solutions:**
- Logical: make elevator faster
- Psychological: add floor-to-ceiling mirrors so people are distracted
- Logical: make it cheaper
- Psychological: make fewer of them and raise the price — people want it more

Business owners must increasingly seek psychological solutions, not logical ones. Most logical solutions have already been tried.

### The Higher Price = Higher Value Paradox

In blind taste tests of identical wine at different price points, tasters consistently rated the more expensive wine as better. Higher price increases perceived value without changing the product.

"When you raise your price, you increase the value the consumer receives without changing anything else about your product."

When prices are high enough, prospects think: "This is so much more expensive, there must be something entirely different going on here." That's how you create a category of one.

People who pay the most also:
- Are more emotionally invested
- Follow through more
- Achieve better results
- Cost less to fulfill (they show up, do the work)
- Give better referrals
- Complain less

## How to Apply

**Step 1: Map your current offer against all four drivers**

For each driver, score your offer 0-10:
- Dream Outcome clarity (1-10)
- Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (1-10)
- Time Delay (10 = instant, 1 = very long)
- Effort & Sacrifice (10 = effortless, 1 = extremely demanding)

**Step 2: Identify the lowest scores — those are your biggest opportunities**

**Step 3: Increase Dream Outcome**
- Are you selling the destination or the plane ride? Stop selling the membership. Sell the weight loss.
- Reframe all benefits in terms of what life looks like after
- Use status language: how will others perceive them?
- Get specific: "Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks" vs "lose weight"

**Step 4: Increase Perceived Likelihood of Achievement**
- Stack proof (see hormozi-proof skill for the 13-point proof checklist)
- Add guarantees that reverse risk
- Communicate track record explicitly
- Show your process and method (process = credibility)
- Add specificity to every claim

**Step 5: Decrease Time Delay**
- Engineer a "fast win" in the first 7 days
- Build visible milestones into the customer journey
- Add components that deliver instant value (templates, done-for-you pieces)
- Make the path to the result visible

**Step 6: Decrease Effort & Sacrifice**
- Audit every step they must take — remove as many as possible
- Convert DIY components to DFY or DWY
- Create systems, templates, and tools that do the work for them
- Eliminate prerequisites they'd otherwise need to acquire

**Step 7: Recalculate and reprice**

After improving all four drivers, your offer is genuinely more valuable. Price accordingly. Price is just the expression of value in the market.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Only working the top half**
Most beginner marketers make bigger and bigger claims (Dream Outcome + Likelihood). These are the easiest to establish and therefore least differentiated. Anyone can promise. The competitive moat is in reducing Time Delay and Effort.

**Mistake 2: Solving the logical problem instead of the psychological one**
Most logical solutions have already been tried. The real opportunity is in psychological solutions that reduce perceived time delay and perceived effort.

**Mistake 3: Assuming prospects understand the value you've added**
You can add real value and have prospects not perceive it. Communication of value is as important as creation of value.

**Mistake 4: Racing to the bottom on price**
Lowering price reduces margins → less budget to deliver → lower results → lower perceived value → more churn → death spiral. "99% of businesses need to raise their prices to grow, not lower them."

**Mistake 5: Skipping fast wins**
The first 7 days after purchase are critical. Without an early win, buyers experience buyer's remorse and disengage. This leads to churn, refunds, and poor results.

**Mistake 6: Confusing "charge a lot" with "rip people off"**
The gap between what they pay (price) and what they get (value) should be massive — in their favor. That's what makes it a good deal at a high price.

## Examples

### Example 1: Gym Membership → Weight Loss Challenge
**Before (gym membership, $99/mo):**
- Dream Outcome: Vague — "get fit"
- Likelihood: Low — no structure, no accountability
- Time Delay: 12+ months
- Effort: High daily commitment

**After (Lose 20lbs in 6 Weeks Challenge, $599):**
- Dream Outcome: Specific — "lose 20lbs"
- Likelihood: High — structured program, track record, guarantee
- Time Delay: 6 weeks (clear endpoint)
- Effort: Provided meal plans, grocery lists, workouts, accountability

Result: Sold far more units at 6x the price.

### Example 2: Consulting → Gym Launch (Done-With-You)
**Before:** Alex flew to gyms for 21 days, did everything himself
**After:** Done-with-you remote model at $16,000 for 16 weeks (vs. $5,000 competitor, vs. $500 lowest-price)

Priced at 3x the highest-priced competitor, 32x the cheapest. Still sold because:
- Dream Outcome: +$239,000/year (documented with data)
- Likelihood: 4,000+ gyms proven track record
- Time Delay: Results visible within 7 days (first sale)
- Effort: They did it from home with support

### Example 3: Photography ($300 → $1,500, 5x increase)
Using Value Equation levers without changing the product:
- Reframed outcome (status: "prints that become family heirlooms")
- Added guarantee (100% reshoot if not delighted)
- Added same-day preview (reduced time delay)
- Created session prep guide (reduced effort)

Result: 5x price increase → 38x profit increase (from $1k/week to $38k/week).

### Example 4: Meditation vs. Xanax (Same Dream Outcome)
Both deliver: relaxation, decreased anxiety, feelings of well-being.

| Driver | Meditation | Xanax |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Dream Outcome | Same | Same |
| Likelihood | Low (requires skill) | High (pharmaceutical) |
| Time Delay | Months | Minutes |
| Effort | High (daily practice) | Low (swallow pill) |

Result: Xanax is a multi-billion dollar product. Meditation businesses are rare at scale. Same dream outcome, vastly different value perception on the other three drivers.
