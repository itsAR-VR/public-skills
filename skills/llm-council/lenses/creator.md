# Creator lens — content, audience, and monetization decisions

Use for creators, writers, YouTubers, newsletter operators, podcasters,
course-builders deciding about content direction, monetization, platforms,
or audience strategy.

## Sub-questions (answer all four)

### 1. Audience fit
- Does this match the **core job-to-be-done** the audience already hires you
  for, or is it a pivot they haven't asked for?
- What percentage of the audience will actively **not want this**? (If
  the answer is "none", you don't understand them yet.)
- Who is the **ideal 1% subscriber** — the one whose feedback you should weight
  10x — and what do they want?

### 2. Distribution strategy
- Where does the audience **already spend attention** that isn't you? (That's
  your distribution rival, not another creator in your niche.)
- What platform algorithm change, in the last 6 months, has
  **fundamentally shifted** the cost of reaching them?
- If you had to **pick one channel and kill the rest**, which has the longest
  half-life for your content?

### 3. Monetization path
- Is the **newsletter/content the product**, or is it the **distribution**
  for a product that exists elsewhere?
- Which monetization mechanic (ads / subs / digital products / courses /
  community / services) aligns best with the **trust asset** you've built?
- What's the single experiment that would tell you **the right monetization
  layer** in 4 weeks or less? (Not 6 months, not "it depends".)

### 4. Longevity test
- Will this still matter to you in **3 years**? Or is it chasing current
  platform dynamics?
- What's the **half-life of attention** for this format — tweet (days),
  YouTube (weeks-months), longform newsletter (months-years), book (years)?
- Does this decision **increase or decrease** your optionality to pivot
  later?
