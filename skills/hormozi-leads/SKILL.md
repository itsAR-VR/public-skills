# Hormozi Lead Generation & Nurture

You are an expert in Hormozi's lead generation and lead nurture systems. You understand how to get strangers to want to buy your stuff using the Core 4 framework, and how to maximize show rates using the Four Pillars of Lead Nurture. You know that "if they do not show, they cannot buy."

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Building or fixing a lead generation system from scratch
- Diagnosing low show rates or appointment completion rates
- Choosing between warm outreach, cold outreach, content, or paid advertising
- Setting up lead nurture sequences to get more people to show up
- Figuring out how to get strangers to raise their hand and express interest
- Creating lead magnets or free value to attract prospects
- Scaling beyond word-of-mouth to predictable new customer flow

## Core Frameworks

### The Advertising Equation

**Leads = Advertising × Offer**

You increase leads by:
1. Reaching more people (more advertising volume or channels)
2. Making a better offer (more compelling reason to raise their hand)

Advertising merely lets people know about your stuff. The offer makes them want to take action. Bad offer + lots of advertising = lots of money wasted.

### The Core 4 — Lead Generation Methods

Hormozi's four levers to get strangers interested in your stuff:

**1. Warm Outreach** — Reach out to people you already know
- Your existing network, past clients, referrals, warm contacts
- Highest conversion rate of any method
- Most businesses have more warm leads than they realize
- Tactic: DM/text/call every person in your phone who could benefit. Be direct, not salesy
- Script: "Hey [name], I'm helping [avatar] achieve [result]. Do you know anyone who might want that?" Then ask if they want it too
- Fastest path to first dollar

**2. Cold Outreach** — Reach out to people you don't know
- Email, DM, cold calling, LinkedIn
- Volume game: more contacts = more responses
- Personalization dramatically increases response rate
- The Law of Threes: 3 lines → who it's for, what you do, what to do next
- "If they can't understand it in 3 seconds, they won't read it"
- Works best when paired with a specific, relevant reason for contacting them

**3. Content** — Post stuff that attracts people to you
- Long-form content (YouTube, podcast, blog) compounds over time
- Short-form content (Instagram Reels, TikToks) gets volume fast
- The Content Cycle: create → post → engage with people who engage → convert engaged people to leads
- Content is delayed gratification — 6-12+ months to see compounding returns
- But once built, lowers CAC permanently

**4. Paid Advertising** — Pay to show your stuff to strangers
- Fastest way to scale lead volume
- The Advertising Equation: Impression → Click → Lead → Sale
- Paid ads amplify what's already working organically
- Start with what's working organically, then pour gasoline on it with paid
- Test many creatives; most will fail; winners scale

### Lead Magnets — Getting Strangers to Raise Their Hand

A lead magnet is a free piece of value that gets a stranger to exchange their contact info. The formula:

**Lead Magnet = [Specific Result] for [Specific Avatar] in [Specific Timeframe] without [Specific Sacrifice]**

Examples:
- "Free checklist: 5 things gym owners do to get 10 new members in 30 days"
- "Free video: how to close cold leads without being pushy"
- "Free audit: see exactly why your ads aren't converting"

**Lead Magnet Types:**
1. **Checklists** — quick, actionable, scannable
2. **Templates** — give them something they can use immediately
3. **Tools/Calculators** — interactive = high perceived value
4. **Mini-courses** — good for high-ticket, longer buying cycle
5. **Free trial/sample** — product speaks for itself
6. **Free consultation/audit** — highest conversion to sale; hardest to scale

**The Key:** Lead magnets must solve a real, specific problem for your exact avatar. Generic = worthless. Specific = magnetic.

## The Four Pillars of Lead Nurture

Once someone becomes a lead, your job is to get them to show up to the appointment. Hormozi built the ALAN software and analyzed 4,000+ appointments per day. The data showed four drivers of show rates:

**Throughput = Schedule Rate × Show Rate**
- 100 leads × 50% schedule rate = 50 scheduled
- 50 scheduled × 50% show rate = 25 shows
- Throughput = 25% (25 shows out of 100 leads)

A 20-40% increase in throughput = 2-3x your profit (if running 20% margins).

---

### Pillar I: Availability — "If leads cannot schedule, they do not show."

The single biggest variable in show rates (per Hormozi's data). More available appointment slots = more shows.

**What to do:**
1. **Take appointments all 7 days per week** — you pay rent 7 days, sell 7 days
2. **Take appointments more hours per day** — 6am–6pm PST covers the highest US purchase window
3. **Give leads more flexible time slots** — offer every 15 minutes instead of every 30/60 minutes
4. **Have all 3 scheduling options:** inbound (they call you), outbound (you call them), self-scheduling (online)
5. **Eliminate friction** from your scheduler — every extra step kills conversions

**Self-scheduler checklist:**
- Header confirms they're in the right place
- Available dates/times visible immediately on page load
- Works on mobile
- Pre-fill any data they already gave you
- Remove every unnecessary field

**When to add friction:** If you're getting too many bad-fit appointments, add a short video or sales letter before the scheduler, or require a short application.

---

### Pillar II: Speed — "Money loves speed. Wealth loves time. Poverty loves indecision."

**The Stats:**
- 391% increase in conversions when leads are contacted within 60 seconds (Velocify)
- 7x more likely to qualify a lead called within 1 hour vs 1 hour later (Harvard Business Review)
- 78% of customers buy from the first company to respond (Lead Connect)

**Three types of speed:**

**1. Speed to First Contact**
- Target: contact leads within 5 minutes of opt-in. Goal: under 60 seconds.
- The faster you contact, the fewer follow-ups you need
- Litmus test: If you're not getting "wow, that was fast!" at least once a day, you're not fast enough

**2. Speed to First Appointment**
- Book appointments within 72 hours max (same day, next day, day after)
- The closer the appointment, the higher the show rate
- Pull appointments forward whenever possible
- Same-day appointments have 100% "show rate" when on the phone live

**The Hot Handoff Script:**
> "Hey, this is Jim from XYZ. I saw you booked for Monday. Calling to confirm some details. Is now a terrible time? [Who they are, what they want, what they've struggled with]. Great — I actually have a cancellation today at 4pm. Want to get squared away now?"

**3. Speed of Response**
- After appointment is booked, respond to messages fast
- Leads make buying decisions before the call based on your responsiveness
- Use timestamps. Track response time by rep. If show rates drop, check response speeds first.

---

### Pillar III: Personalization — "Personalize, don't pressure."

The more relevant and personal the communication, the more likely leads respond and show up.

**Six personalization tactics:**

**1. Use their preferred communication channel**
- Start contact on every channel (call, text, DM, email)
- Continue conversation wherever they first respond
- Follow-up across multiple platforms like you'd follow up with your own parents

**2. Qualify leads first**
- Use applications or pre-call forms to filter
- Cancel appointments with unqualified leads — this counterintuitively increases revenue
- Focus time on the best-fit prospects

**3. Send best leads to best closers**
- Score leads 1-5 or red/yellow/green
- Route your highest-scored leads to your top closers
- A timeshare rep who implemented this 5x'd office production; company went from $200M → $1B

**4. Segment your messaging**
- Easy: Research each lead for 5 minutes before contact. Reference what you found
  > "Hey Greg, I saw your site traffic is down 18% this quarter. We had another auto shop in the same situation — they're up 100% 90 days later. I prepped a walkthrough..."
- Hard: Build separate nurture sequences for each lead category (by revenue, pain, desired outcome, etc.)

**5. Incentivize showing up**
- "Push" incentive (gift before they show): Send $5 coffee gift card — "I wanted to make sure you're caffeinated for our call." They get it regardless of whether they show. Creates reciprocity.
- "Pull" incentive (A/B choice): "Do you want the black shirt or the white one?" Both choices assume they'll show. Any binary choice works (start dates, payment type, sizes, colors, media format).

**6. Show personalized proof**
- Match proof to the lead's demographics and situation
- "If talking to an older white male professional, show him results from other older white male professionals"
- Send proof: after scheduling, between calls, every 90 days if they ghost
- See `hormozi-proof` for full proof stacking system

### Pillar IV: Volume — "Volume negates luck."

Nearly half of all salespeople give up after one attempt. Meanwhile, leads often need multiple touchpoints.

**The "Garbage Man" Principle (Jacob's Story):**
- Jacob took all the "yellow" (less-qualified) leads the senior reps didn't want
- He gave them full effort during off-hours and weekends
- Rose to #1 salesperson on a team of 26, including senior grown men
- "Yellows are the new gold." Every lead is free practice.
- "Being a garbage man" became the cultural ideal — no lead judging, maximum volume

**Scheduling Volume Sequence:**
1. Call within 5 minutes of opt-in
2. Double-dial immediately (many phones block first calls)
3. Leave voicemail
4. Text immediately after voicemail
5. Call + text twice more on day 1 (space out by a few hours)
6. Call twice for next 2 days (once AM, once PM; text after second call)
7. Call + text once per day for next 4 days
8. After day 7 → transition to long-term nurture (free value + soft calls to action every 30-90 days)

**Reminder Volume (once scheduled):**
- **Automated:** Immediately (confirmation), 24 hours out, 12 hours out, 3 hours out
  - Always include: date, time, who they'll speak with, area code/number you'll call from
  - Use their local time zone
- **Manual (real cell phone):** Night before, morning of, 60 minutes before

**BAMFAM — Book a Meeting From a Meeting**
- Never end a call with "I'll circle back" or "let me propose some times"
- Book the next appointment live, while you're on the call
- "Someone being dodgy about the next call is an objection. Handle it."
- Internally, treat BAMFAM as a way of life — a cultural expectation

## How to Apply

### Diagnosing a Leads Problem

**Step 1: Find your bottleneck**
- Low lead volume → fix advertising (Core 4)
- Low schedule rate → fix availability (pillar 1) and speed (pillar 2)
- Low show rate → fix personalization (pillar 3), volume (pillar 4), and speed of response
- Low close rate → see `hormozi-closing` skill

**Step 2: Calculate your throughput**
```
Throughput % = (Total Shows) / (Total Leads Expressed Interest) × 100
```
If <30%, you have a nurture problem. If <10%, you have a severe availability/speed problem.

**Step 3: Fix in order**
1. Availability (biggest single lever)
2. Speed to first contact
3. Speed to first appointment
4. Personalization + segmentation
5. Volume + BAMFAM

### Building a Lead System from Zero

**Month 1:** Start with warm outreach only. Contact every person you've ever met. DM, text, call.
**Month 2:** Add cold outreach. Build a list. Send 50-100 personalized messages per day.
**Month 3:** Add content creation. Pick one platform. Post daily.
**Month 4+:** If you're profitable, turn on paid ads to amplify what's working organically.

### Culture of Execution

Hormozi emphasizes that execution is the hardest part. Create cultural norms around:

- **Speed is king** — reward the fastest responders
- **Volume negates luck** — celebrate high contact volume
- **Yellows are the new gold** — no judging leads, equal effort for all
- **BAMFAM as a way of life** — next appointment always booked in current meeting
- **Process > All** — no one is above the process

**Track three metrics per rep:**
1. Show rate (measures work ethic)
2. Close rate (measures skill)
3. Lead-to-close rate (measures holistic performance)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Giving up after 1-2 attempts** — Industry data: 44% of salespeople stop after the first attempt. The answer: 7+ touches
2. **Slow first contact** — average business takes 42 hours to respond. Average response time should be under 5 minutes
3. **Limited availability** — running Mon-Fri 9-5 means leaving 40%+ of revenue on the table
4. **One channel only** — using only email or only phone; use all channels, continue on whichever they respond to
5. **Generic nurture** — same message to all leads; segment by avatar pain, revenue, desired outcome
6. **No BAMFAM** — letting leads go to "no man's land" without a next scheduled touchpoint
7. **Cherry-picking good leads** — ignoring yellow/unqualified leads costs free practice reps
8. **Starting paid ads before organic works** — paid amplifies what works; if organic doesn't work, paid burns money

## Examples

### Lead Nurture in Action: The Nail Salon Test
Leila called 4 salons looking for a same-day appointment:
- Salon 1: No answer
- Salon 2: "Nothing until tomorrow" — she said no
- Salon 3: "Let me call you back" — she said no
- Salon 4: "Yes, walk-ins welcome. First name? I texted your confirmation — see you soon, Nancy's ready."

Salon 4 wins simply because of availability. All other skill is irrelevant if you can't schedule them.

### The Gift Card Push
> "Hey [name]! I wanted to make sure you're caffeinated for our call tomorrow. Check your email — I just sent you a $5 Starbucks gift card."

Show rate result: near 100% for anyone who receives a personalized gift before the appointment. Reciprocity is automatic even when prospects know it's intentional.

### A/B Pull Incentive for a Beauty Brand
Prospect opts in → called within 5 minutes → "Hey, since you're a first-time client, I'm setting aside some free products. Do you want face cream or hand lotion?"
- They pick face cream
- "Perfect, I have it waiting for you tomorrow."
- Show rate for anyone who answers an A/B question: extremely high

### Segmented Proof Delivery
- Older male professional lead → send him before/afters from other older male professionals
- Female weight loss lead → send her case study from similar woman, similar age, similar weight
- B2B lead → show business revenue growth from similar-sized businesses in same niche
- If they ghost → send results from people who signed up around when they ghosted ("you could have had this by now")
