# Hormozi Skill: Pricing

Use when: margins are thin, growth is unprofitable, or price changes feel risky.

## Core principles
- Price for **total return/LTV**, not just first-sale conversion.
- Higher price can increase profit even with lower close rate.
- Value-based pricing outperforms copying competitor pricing.
- Use anchors and billing design (display vs charge cadence) to improve economics.
- Price raises need structure and communication, not random announcements.

## Key frameworks
### 1) Three Models of Pricing
1. **Cost Plus Pricing**
2. **Competitor Based Pricing**
3. **Value Based Pricing** (preferred in source)

### 2) Value-Driven Pricing Metrics
Evaluate price changes with:
- Conversion rate at each price
- Churn/repurchase behavior at each price
- LTV / total return

### 3) Pricing operating rules (selected from playbook)
- High close rate often implies room to raise price.
- Full capacity often implies room to raise price.
- Keep raising until extra revenue no longer offsets lost sales.
- Bill less frequently to reduce churn (monthly < quarterly < annual churn tendency).
- Display in small increments, bill on longer increments when appropriate.

### 4) Price Anchoring
- First number anchors perception.
- Offer premium option or annual/prepay paths to reframe value.
- Use discount ladders intentionally (not constant discounting).

### 5) Price Raise RAISE framework
- **R**: Remind them of value already delivered.
- **A**: Address the price change directly.
- **I**: Invest in their future (what improves).
- **S**: Soften with loyalty reward/expiring discount.
- **E**: Explain concerns and handle objections.

Playbook constraints:
- Don’t grandfather everyone by default.
- Avoid lifetime deals as standard model.
- Test raises on new customers before full rollout.

### 6) Fast Cash pricing motion (for immediate cash)
From checklist emphasis:
- Build high-value offer to past/present customers.
- Set bold pricing (source notes 10x–50x ranges in context of campaign).
- Run tight deadline campaign.
- After delivery, collect **4Rs**: Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells.

## Practical templates
### Template A: Price Test Table
- Price A: conv `[ ]` churn `[ ]` LTV `[ ]` total return `[ ]`
- Price B: conv `[ ]` churn `[ ]` LTV `[ ]` total return `[ ]`
- Decision: choose highest total return unless strategic exception.

### Template B: RAISE letter skeleton
`Over [period], you got [results]. Starting [date], price changes from [old] to [new]. We’re also adding [future value]. As a thank-you, you keep [expiring loyalty incentive] until [date]. If you have concerns, reply and we’ll walk options.`

### Template C: Annual prepay offer
`Current monthly is [X]. If you prepay annual, it’s [Y] (includes [incentive]).`

## Examples
1. Capacity-limited service:
- Raised prices, accepted lower close rate, net profit increased.

2. Subscription:
- Added annual prepay option with modest discount; cash flow + LTV improved.

3. Fast cash cycle:
- 7-day campaign to existing base with clear deadline and consult CTA.
