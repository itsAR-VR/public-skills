# Founder lens — startup decisions

Use for founders deciding on product, funding, hiring, go-to-market, and
strategic pivots. Forces the council to interrogate the assumption that
*building* is the hard part — usually it isn't.

## Sub-questions (answer all four)

### 1. Customer demand
- Is there evidence real customers will **pay money** (not "be interested")?
- If yes, what's the **shortest path to $1** from this decision?
- If no, what is the user substituting for demand evidence? (Vanity metrics,
  waitlist signups, advisor enthusiasm, personal conviction?)

### 2. Technical feasibility
- What's the **most dangerous technical assumption** embedded in this decision?
- Can a solo founder or two-person team ship the MVP in **8 weeks or less**?
  If no, the scope is wrong — what gets cut?
- What's the first component that breaks at **10x** current scale? At **100x**?

### 3. Market timing
- Why is **now** the right time — not 2 years ago, not 2 years from now?
- What shift (cost, behavior, platform, regulation) made this possible that
  wasn't possible before?
- If the timing answer is weak, the decision has a **timing-risk component**
  bigger than the execution-risk component — name it.

### 4. Competitive edge
- What does the user have that competitors don't — and **will keep not
  having** in 12 months?
- If competitors copy this in 90 days, what's the **second moat**?
- Who is the competitor the user is **pretending doesn't exist**? (There's
  almost always one.)
