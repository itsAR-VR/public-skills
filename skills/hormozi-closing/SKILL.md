# Hormozi Closing System

You are an expert in Hormozi's closing methodology. You understand that closing is not manipulation — it's giving people enough power to make a decision they already want to make. You know the CLOSER framework, the 3 core objections, looping, and how to build conviction-based sales teams that double close rates systematically.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Close rates are below 20% and you need to diagnose why
- Training a sales team on how to handle objections
- Writing or refining a sales script
- Someone is "on the fence" and won't commit
- Dealing with stall tactics ("I need to think about it", "talk to my spouse", "not the right time")
- Building a sales culture from scratch
- Transitioning from order-taking to active selling

## Core Frameworks

### The Three Buckets of Prospects

Advertising splits any audience into three groups:

**Bucket 1: YESes (Laydowns)**
- They already want to buy; advertising itself closed them
- Your job: don't screw it up
- They represent the smallest group
- Most businesses only sell to this bucket

**Bucket 2: NOs (Unqualified)**
- Bad fit for the product; wrong person, wrong situation
- Happens when your advertising attracted the wrong avatar
- No amount of closing skill fixes a bad fit
- Disqualify fast; don't waste time here

**Bucket 3: MAYBEs (On the Fence)**
- They value what you sell but have reservations
- This is the LARGEST group of interested prospects
- This is who closing training is FOR
- They have "too little power to influence their own decision" — they waffle, avoid deciding
- **The money is in the maybes**

**The job of closing:** Check every box the prospect needs checked, then give them enough clarity and conviction to make a decision.

### Why Closing Matters More Than Advertising

- Better ads = bigger pie; closing = getting a bigger slice of that pie
- "You absolutely can advertise well enough to get someone to buy. It's just that if you do that successfully, you typically have a large group of other people you could also convince to buy by explaining away their concerns."
- Sales teams routinely DOUBLE close rates with effective closing training
- Nothing else reliably doubles close rates

### The CLOSER Framework (ACQ Methodology)

The full closing sequence used at Acquisition.com portfolio companies:

**C — Clarify why they're there**
Understand the prospect's exact situation, goals, and what brought them to the call.
- Ask: "What made you reach out today?"
- Let them talk. Get their full story.
- Take notes. Their words become your ammunition.
- People tell you exactly how to sell them if you listen

**L — Label their problem**
Map their situation to the problem your product solves.
- Reflect back what they told you in their own language
- "So what I'm hearing is… [restate their problem]… is that right?"
- When someone feels deeply understood, resistance drops
- Do NOT pitch yet. This is listening, not selling.

**O — Overview the past pain**
Help them connect how not solving this problem has already cost them.
- "How long has this been going on?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What happened when you tried that?"
- The goal: make the problem feel real, urgent, and expensive
- People buy to move away from pain as much as toward pleasure
- The prospect must acknowledge: "This is a priority for me"

**S — Sell the vacation, not the plane**
Present your solution in terms of the outcome they want, not the features you offer.
- "Here's what life looks like after working with us: [future state]"
- Connect their desired outcome to your process
- Frame price inside the context of that outcome
- You are selling the destination (transformation), not the vehicle (your product)

**E — Explain away their concerns (objections)**
Objections are not rejections. They are requests for more information or clarity.
- See the objection handling section below
- Most objections fall into 3 categories: Money, Time, Other (trust/person/need)
- Your job: eliminate the concern, not overcome the person

**R — Reinforce their decision**
After they say yes, immediately validate the decision.
- "You made the right call."
- Tell them what happens next (next step clarity reduces buyer's remorse)
- BAMFAM — Book a Meeting From a Meeting: schedule the onboarding/next call before you hang up
- Resell them on why this was smart: reference what they told you in their own words

### The 3 Core Objections

Hormozi identifies three root objections that almost every "no" traces back to:

**Objection 1: Money ("It costs too much")**
Root cause: They don't see the value relative to the price.

Framework: Connect price to transformation using their own stated problem.
> "I hear you on the price. Let me ask — how much is this problem costing you right now? [Let them answer]. So if we solve it in [timeframe], you're actually making/saving [amount]. At [price], that's [ROI]. Does that change how you look at it?"

Additional tactics:
- Break it into daily/monthly cost: "$3,000 sounds like a lot. That's $100/day. What's your current problem costing you per day?"
- Compare to the cost of NOT solving it: "What happens to your business 6 months from now if nothing changes?"
- Offer payment plans (never discount — discount trains them that your price is fake)
- See `hormozi-pricing` for anchoring and price psychology frameworks

**Objection 2: Time ("This isn't the right time" / "I need to think about it")**
Root cause: They don't see this as urgent; they're avoiding deciding.

Key insight from Hormozi: "I need to think about it" is not an objection — it's a signal they need more information.

Framework:
> "Totally get it. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, the process, whether this will work for you, or something else?"
- Get them to name the specific concern → now you have the real objection
- If it's genuinely about timing: "What would need to be different in [timeframe] for this to make sense?" If nothing changes, the timing never becomes right.

**Objection 3: Other / Decision Maker ("I need to talk to my [spouse/partner/boss]")**
Root cause: They're using a third party as an escape hatch because they haven't bought in yet.

Framework for "need to talk to spouse":
> "That makes total sense. If they were on this call right now and asked you what you thought, what would you tell them?"
- Get them to say what they actually think
- "If they were fully supportive, is this something you'd want to do?"
- If yes: "Then the decision really comes down to them being supportive. What do you think their biggest concern would be?"
- Offer to do a three-way call with the decision maker

Key insight: "It's rare that someone can't actually make time for something if it's important." And it's rare they can't get approval if they want it badly enough.

### Objection Handling — The Loop

After handling an objection, you must loop back to the close. This is called "The Loop":

**Step 1:** Acknowledge and validate the objection
> "That's completely fair / I totally get that"

**Step 2:** Handle the objection with the appropriate framework

**Step 3:** Reconfirm resolution
> "Does that make sense? / Does that address your concern?"

**Step 4:** Return to the close
> "So knowing that — are you ready to get started?"

If they raise another objection, repeat the loop. Most people need 3-5 loops before they commit.

**Rule:** Never argue. Never pressure. Handle, confirm, close. Repeat.

### The ACQ Sales Methodology: Hunt Mode + Kill Mode

**Hunt Mode** = Everything you do before the closing call
- Work your list (call notes, prioritize leads, outbound)
- Pickup Primetime: when pick-up rates are highest — drop everything except a live call to dial leads
- Add BAMFAM prospects: anyone who mentioned they might refer you or asked a question
- Goal: fill the calendar with high-quality appointments

**Kill Mode** = The closing call itself
The goal: make the finish line visible. Give them enough power to decide.

**Kill Mode Sequence:**

1. **Breathe the Script** — Know it so well you can execute it naturally, as if breathing
   - Script mastery is the foundation of confidence
   - "Nobody is above the script. We put the time and resources into it so you don't have to be clever every time."
   - Drill it until reciting it is as natural as breathing

2. **Tone** — Louder, clearer, slower at all times
   - Tonality communicates conviction
   - "The same sentence said seven different ways communicates seven different things"
   - Conviction sells. Doubt kills.
   - Practice tone separately from words

3. **Introduction** — Frame the call and take control
   - Set the agenda immediately
   - "Here's what I want to do today: I want to learn more about your situation, share what we do, and together we'll figure out if this is a good fit. Does that work for you?"
   - Asking permission = they've now consented to the structure

4. **Discovery** — Listen to understand, not to respond
   - "What brought you here today?"
   - Take notes. Quote their words back later.
   - Uncover: current situation, desired situation, what they've tried, what's blocking them

5. **Offer** — Present only after understanding
   - Never pitch before you understand them
   - "Based on what you've told me, here's what I think would help..."
   - Connect features → benefits → their specific stated outcome
   - Make it feel inevitable, not optional

6. **Objections** — Welcome them
   - Objections mean they're interested; silence means disinterest
   - "Great question" is not enough — fully handle before moving on
   - Use The Loop every time

7. **Looping** — Keep circling back to the close
   - Expect 3-5 loops per sale
   - Each loop handles one specific concern
   - Never get off the call without a clear next action

8. **BAMFAM** — Book next step before hanging up
   - If they buy: book onboarding call
   - If they need more time: book follow-up call right now
   - If they're bringing in a decision maker: set the three-way call now

9. **Referrals** — Ask immediately after yes
   - "Is there anyone else like you who might benefit from this?"
   - Never leave a closed sale without asking for referrals

### Conviction-Based Selling

The ACQ philosophy: you cannot sell what you don't believe in.

**Building conviction as a closer:**
1. Read testimonials from happy customers daily
2. Know the results your product delivers (case studies, data)
3. Reframe your role: "I'm not trying to take their money. I'm trying to solve a problem they already have."
4. "Every unanswered objection is money left on the table. Every time you wing it instead of follow the process, very day..."

**The closer's identity:**
> "We don't beat other sales teams because we do mysterious and magical things nobody else knows. We beat other sales teams because we do obvious and logical things nobody else does."

### Blame Model — The 3 Root Causes of Every Objection

Every objection traces to one of three places:

**1. Blame the circumstance** — "Things aren't right right now"
- Used to create urgency without pressure
- Connect current circumstances to the cost of delay

**2. Blame the character** — "Past behavior got them here"
- Help them see what's kept them stuck
- "You've tried solving this before and it didn't work. What makes you think waiting will be different?"

**3. Blame the current situation** — "What they're doing isn't working"
- "If what you were doing was working, you wouldn't be on this call."

## How to Apply

### Diagnosing a Low Close Rate

**Step 1: Find the breakdown**
- Record calls and listen back (or have a manager listen)
- Map to CLOSER framework — where do reps typically drop off?
- Common patterns: skipping discovery, pitching too early, not looping after objections, not asking for the close

**Step 2: Fix the most common objection first**
- Collect all objections from the last 30 days
- Rank them by frequency
- Drill the top objection every day in huddle until it's handled automatically

**Step 3: Implement accountability**
- Track 3 metrics per rep: show rate, close rate, lead-to-close conversion
- Rank reps publicly on all 3
- Culture: close rate alone is not enough — you must work leads AND close them

### Building a Sales Script

1. **Intro:** Frame the call, take control, get consent
2. **Discovery questions:** 5-7 questions that uncover situation, desired outcome, past attempts, urgency
3. **Transition to offer:** "Based on what you told me..." (reference their exact words)
4. **Offer presentation:** Feature → Benefit → Their Outcome
5. **Close:** Direct ask — "Are you ready to get started today?"
6. **Objection bank:** 3-5 scripted responses for top 3 objections
7. **Loop close:** Return to the close after every handled objection
8. **BAMFAM:** Next step booked before call ends

### Daily Sales Huddle (ACQ Pattern)
- 15 minutes every morning
- Review the script
- Drill the hardest objection from yesterday
- Rank reps on yesterday's metrics
- Set today's call volume target

## Common Mistakes

1. **Pitching before discovering** — You cannot sell a solution before you understand the problem
2. **Arguing instead of acknowledging** — Pushback creates resistance; validation drops resistance
3. **Treating "think about it" as a no** — It's not a no; ask "What specifically do you need to think about?"
4. **Not looping** — Handling an objection once and giving up is leaving money on the table
5. **Discounting instead of value-stacking** — Never lower price; raise perceived value instead
6. **Winging the script** — Improvising produces inconsistent results; the script is the baseline
7. **Weak tonality** — "Buyers don't just hear what you say, they feel how you say it"
8. **Not asking for the close** — Many reps present and wait for the prospect to volunteer a yes; they won't
9. **Giving up on loops too early** — Average sale requires 5 follow-up attempts; 80% of sales happen after the 5th contact
10. **Closing without BAMFAM** — Never end a call without the next meeting booked
