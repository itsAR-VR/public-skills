# Welcome Series

## Overview

**Purpose:** Establish expectations, build brand confidence, and guide first-time subscribers toward their first purchase.

**Trigger:** New subscriber added to list (opt-in form, lead magnet, checkout opt-in, pop-up)

**Goal:** Convert subscriber to first-time buyer. Everything here builds trust — not sell on first contact.

**Length:** 5 emails over 10-14 days

**Exit condition:** Any purchase → move to Post-Purchase flow immediately. Remove from welcome on purchase.

**CVR target:** 8-15% of subscribers → first purchase

---

## Email Sequence

### Email 1 — Send immediately after signup
**Subject angle:** Warm welcome + deliver the promise (discount code, lead magnet, or what they signed up for)
**Preview text:** Reinforce the benefit, create anticipation

**Copy angle:**
- Deliver what was promised instantly (discount code, free guide, etc.)
- Brief brand introduction — who you are, why you exist, what you stand for
- Set expectations: what they'll hear from you and how often
- One clear CTA: use the code / download / shop now

**CTA:** Use your [X]% off / Shop now / Download your guide

**Tone:** Warm, personal, excited — like a handshake

---

### Email 2 — Day 2
**Subject angle:** What makes you different (differentiation email)
**Preview text:** Tease the key proof point or ingredient/process

**Copy angle:**
- Address the obvious question: "Why you vs. everyone else?"
- Highlight your #1 differentiator (ingredient, process, mission, sourcing)
- Use a short story or origin moment to make it human
- Social proof signal (customer count, press mention, certification)
- Soft CTA: invite them to explore, not to buy yet

**CTA:** See how we're different / Learn more / Read our story

---

### Email 3 — Day 4
**Subject angle:** Social proof / bestsellers (what everyone's buying)
**Preview text:** "X customers can't be wrong" or number-anchoring

**Copy angle:**
- Feature your top 2-3 bestsellers with brief descriptions
- Customer reviews embedded (2-3 specific, outcome-based quotes)
- "People like you" framing — who buys this and why
- The discount code reminder (if applicable) — don't let it expire silently

**CTA:** Shop bestsellers / See what [X] customers love

---

### Email 4 — Day 7
**Subject angle:** Soft urgency — the discount is expiring / value-driven nudge
**Preview text:** "Your [X]% off expires in [X] days"

**Copy angle:**
- Reminder that the welcome offer won't last forever
- Objection handling: shipping info, return policy, guarantee
- Answer the hesitation — what stops first-time buyers? Address it directly
- Reinforce the risk-free angle (free returns, satisfaction guarantee)

**CTA:** Claim your discount before it expires

---

### Email 5 — Day 10-14
**Subject angle:** Last call / final nudge
**Preview text:** "This is the last time I'll mention it"

**Copy angle:**
- Brief, direct — don't over-explain
- Acknowledge they may not be ready now (remove pressure)
- The offer disappears after this email
- Leave the door open: "We'll still be here when you're ready"
- Include a secondary ask if no purchase: follow on social, join community, read a piece of content

**CTA:** Use your discount now / Shop before it's gone

---

## Suppression Logic

- Suppress anyone who purchases at any point in the sequence
- Suppress anyone who has already been through a welcome flow (returning subscriber)
- Do not add customers who purchased before subscribing — send them to post-purchase instead

---

## A/B Test Priorities

1. Email 1: Discount code vs. no discount (value-led welcome vs. offer-led welcome)
2. Email 3: Bestseller grid vs. single hero product
3. Subject lines: Curiosity vs. direct benefit vs. personalization

---

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Good |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Flow CVR | 8% | 12-15%+ |
| Email 1 open rate | 50%+ | 60-70% |
| Email 1 CTR | 10-15% | 20%+ |
| Unsubscribe rate per email | <0.5% | <0.2% |
