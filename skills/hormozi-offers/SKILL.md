# Hormozi Grand Slam Offers

You are an expert in offer creation using Alex Hormozi's complete Grand Slam Offer system. Your goal is to help users build offers so compelling that prospects feel stupid saying no — at premium prices with premium margins.

## When to Use This Skill

- User wants to create a new offer or product package
- User's current offer isn't converting
- User is getting "too expensive" objections
- User asks what to include in their service
- User wants to bundle products or add bonuses
- User asks about guarantees, scarcity, or urgency tactics
- User wants to name their offer
- User asks how to make something "irresistible"

## Core Frameworks

### The Grand Slam Offer: Core Definition

"An offer so good people feel stupid saying no."

Three things a Grand Slam Offer accomplishes:
1. Solves ALL the perceived problems (not just some)
2. Gives you conviction that what you're selling is one of a kind
3. Makes it impossible to compare or confuse your business with the competition down the street

A Grand Slam Offer is not a commodity. Once you create one, you're no longer competing on price. Prospects make a value-based decision, not a price-based one.

### The Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum

Before building the offer, understand the key tradeoff:
- More you do = easier to sell, harder to fulfill
- Less you do = harder to sell, easier to fulfill

The goal: find the sweet spot where you sell something very well that's also easy to fulfill.

Hormozi's mantra: **"Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction."**
- First: over-deliver to create cash flow
- Second: optimize operations with that cash flow
- Third: raise prices or reduce delivery as you scale

Start by doing too much. It proves your offer works. Then systematize.

### The 5-Step Grand Slam Offer System

#### Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome

Stop selling the product. Sell the destination.

"I wasn't selling my membership anymore. I wasn't selling the plane flight. I was selling the vacation."

Examples of reframing to dream outcomes:
- Gym membership → "Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks"
- Marketing agency → "Get 30 qualified leads in 30 days, guaranteed"
- Accounting software → "Close your books in under 1 hour, every month"
- Coaching program → "Launch your first $10k month in 90 days"

**The dream outcome must:**
- Be specific (include a number + timeframe where possible)
- Describe where they ARRIVE, not what they do
- Connect to an underlying status desire

#### Step 2: List All Problems

Write down every obstacle a prospect faces on the path to their dream outcome — before, during, and after using your product.

Use the four value drivers as a guide for each problem:
1. **Dream Outcome problem** → "It won't be financially worth it"
2. **Likelihood of Achievement problem** → "It won't work for me specifically; external factors will get in my way"
3. **Effort & Sacrifice problem** → "This will be too hard, confusing. I won't like it. I will suck at it."
4. **Time problem** → "This will take too much time. I'm too busy. It will take too long to work."

Think in sequence: What's the FIRST thing they must do? What are all the reasons they can't/won't do that? Then the NEXT thing. And so on.

**Weight loss example: 32-64 total problems from just a few categories:**
- Buying healthy food (hard, time-consuming, expensive, unsustainable)
- Cooking healthy food (same four objections)
- Eating healthy food (same)
- Exercising regularly (same)
- Traveling and staying on plan
- Social situations

"Do not get overwhelmed. This is the best news ever. The more problems you think of, the more problems you get to solve."

Every solved problem = a reason to buy. Every unresolved problem = a reason NOT to buy.

#### Step 3: Solutions List

Turn every problem into a solution. Formula: "How to [reverse the problem] so that [positive outcome]."

Simple flip:
- PROBLEM: Buying healthy food is hard and confusing → SOLUTION: How to make buying healthy food easy and enjoyable, so anyone can do it
- PROBLEM: Cooking takes too much time → SOLUTION: How to cook meals in under 5 minutes
- PROBLEM: Healthy food is expensive → SOLUTION: How to buy healthy food for less than your current grocery bill
- PROBLEM: I can't stick to it while traveling → SOLUTION: How to get healthy food when traveling

Every problem on your list should become a named solution element. This is how the "stack" gets built.

#### Step 4: Create Solution Delivery Vehicles ("The How")

For each solution, brainstorm ALL the ways you could deliver it. Think at three levels:

**One-on-One:**
- In-person sessions
- Personalized plans
- Calls and check-ins
- Text/chat support
- Done-for-you execution

**Small Group:**
- Group coaching calls
- Community access
- Cohort-based programs
- Group accountability

**One-to-Many:**
- Recorded videos/modules
- Templates and tools
- Live webinars
- Software/apps
- Written guides

**Cheat codes for generating delivery vehicle ideas:**
1. What level of personal attention? (1:1, small group, 1:many)
2. What level of effort from them? (DIY, DWY, DFY)
3. What medium? (in-person, phone, email, text, Zoom, chat)
4. If recording: audio, video, or written?
5. Response time: 24/7? 9-5? Within 5 minutes?
6. **The 10x/1/10th test:** If they paid you 10x more, what would you add? If you charged 1/10th and had to make it MORE valuable, how would you do that? This stretches your creativity.

#### Step 5: Trim & Stack

Now eliminate and bundle.

**First cut — Remove:**
- High cost, low value items (bad for you, not much better for them)
- Low cost, low value items (clutter)

**Keep:**
- Low cost to deliver, high value to prospect ← The goldmine
- High cost to deliver, very high value to prospect ← Premium tier

**Stacking:** Bundle the remaining items into the ultimate offer. Assign each a "value" (what they'd pay for this individually elsewhere). Then present the total stack value vs. your price.

**The weight loss bundle example (total stack):***
1. Foolproof Bargain Grocery System — $1,000 value
2. Ready in 5min Busy Parent Cooking Guide — $600 value
3. Personalized Lick Your Fingers Good Meal Plan — $500 value
4. Fat Burning Workouts — $699 value
5. The Ultimate Tone Up While You Travel Blueprint — $199 value
6. The "Never Fall Off" Accountability System — $1,000 value
7. The 'Live It Up While Slimming Down' Eating Out System — $349 value

**Total value: $4,351. Price: $599.**

That's what makes people say "All that? Seriously? Yes, I'm in."

### Enhancing the Offer: The Four Psychological Amplifiers

Once the core offer is built, these four amplifiers increase urgency and desire without changing the offer itself.

#### Amplifier 1: Scarcity

People want what they can't have. People want what other people want. People want things only a select few have access to.

Types of ethical scarcity:
- **Supply scarcity:** Limited number of clients/spots you'll accept ("I can only take 5 new clients this month")
- **Cohort scarcity:** "This cohort closes Friday. Next one isn't until March."
- **Access scarcity:** "This pricing is only available to people who respond in the next 72 hours"

Rule: **Scarcity must be real.** Never create fake scarcity. If you say there are 5 spots and then take anyone who asks, you destroy trust. Make genuine limits and honor them.

Example from the book: Arnold Schwarzenegger's charity fundraiser cut tickets from 200 to 100 and raised the price from $15,000 to $25,000. More demand than ever. One million extra dollars raised before the event started.

#### Amplifier 2: Urgency

Urgency = time-based reason to act now.

Types:
- **Cohort deadline:** "The doors close Friday at midnight"
- **Bonus deadline:** "Order by Thursday and get X bonus included"
- **Price increase:** "This price goes up to $X on [date]"
- **Seasonal:** "We only run this program twice a year"

Urgency answers the question: "Why should I act NOW instead of later?" Every offer should have an answer to this question.

#### Amplifier 3: Bonuses

Bonuses increase perceived value and reduce perceived price. A bonus isn't something you throw in — it's a carefully chosen item that solves one of their remaining objections or adds to the dream outcome.

**The bonus naming formula:** Give every bonus a specific, outcome-focused name.
- Not: "Bonus #1: Grocery Shopping Guide"
- Yes: "Foolproof Bargain Grocery System: How to buy healthy food for less than your current grocery bill and in half the time"

**Bonus stacking:** List each bonus separately with its individual value, then total the stack.

**Types of high-value bonuses:**
- Tools that reduce effort (templates, calculators, trackers)
- Done-for-you assets (pre-made content, plans, scripts)
- Access to expertise (Q&A calls, community, hot seat)
- Accelerators (fast-track versions of slower processes)
- Warranties/insurance (extended guarantees)

**Where bonuses come from:** Arnold's fundraiser bonus: as two bidders competed on an item, Arnold would jump in and add a personal bonus for both — which caused BOTH to increase their bids. A single bonus created two high-value purchases.

#### Amplifier 4: Guarantees

Guarantees reduce risk and increase likelihood of achievement (two value equation drivers).

**Types of guarantees:**

1. **Unconditional Money-Back Guarantee** — "If you're not satisfied for any reason, get a full refund." Highest conversion. Highest refund rate. Best for high-confidence businesses.

2. **Conditional Money-Back Guarantee** — "Do the work, show us your results, and if you don't get X, we refund." Lower conversions than unconditional. Lower refund rate. Better for businesses with specific deliverables.

3. **Anti-Guarantee** — "We offer no refunds. Here's why." Bold. Works when you're supremely confident and your proof is overwhelming. Paradoxically increases perceived quality.

4. **Implied Guarantee** — You don't explicitly state it, but the evidence of your track record implies you stand behind results.

5. **Outcome-Based Guarantee** — "You will get X result or we continue working for free until you do." Extremely powerful. Requires strong delivery.

**Guarantee language template:**
"If you [do the required actions] and don't [specific outcome] within [timeframe], I will [specific remedy]. No questions asked."

Guarantees should directly address the most common objection. If the objection is "What if it doesn't work for me?" → guarantee addresses that exact fear.

#### The Grand Slam Offer Naming Framework

The name of your offer should do two things:
1. Call out the target audience specifically
2. Promise the specific dream outcome with specificity (include timeframe where possible)

**Naming formula:** "[Adjective] [Target Avatar] [Dream Outcome] [Time frame/Qualifier]"

Examples:
- "Gym Owners' 30-Day Revenue Accelerator"
- "The 6-Week Lose 20lbs Challenge for Busy Moms"
- "The Beginner's 90-Day Business Launch Blueprint"
- "E-Commerce Founders' 12-Month Scale to $1M Program"

Bad names: "Gold Package," "Premium Program," "VIP Membership" — these say nothing.

**MAGIC acronym for naming:**
- **M**agnetism — Does it attract the right person?
- **A**vatar — Does it call out the specific target?
- **G**oal — Does it communicate the specific outcome?
- **I**nteresting — Is it memorable and distinct?
- **C**larity — Does a stranger immediately understand what it is?

## How to Apply

**Step 1: Define the dream outcome**
Write it as a specific result with a timeframe. This is what you're selling.

**Step 2: Problem brainstorm (20 minutes)**
List every obstacle between the prospect and the dream outcome. Be exhaustive. Use the four problem buckets (likelihood, time, effort, worth it) for each step.

**Step 3: Solutions brainstorm (20 minutes)**
Flip every problem into a solution. Name each solution.

**Step 4: Delivery vehicles brainstorm (20 minutes)**
For each solution, list every possible way to deliver it. Use 1:1, small group, and 1:many. Use DFY, DWY, and DIY. Use the 10x/1/10th test.

**Step 5: Trim the list**
Remove high cost + low value. Remove low cost + low value. Keep low cost + high value (prioritize these).

**Step 6: Stack and value**
Organize remaining items into a bundled offer. Assign a value to each. Sum the total. Price your offer far below the total stack value.

**Step 7: Add the amplifiers**
- Add one real scarcity element
- Add one real urgency element
- Add 2-4 bonuses that solve lingering objections
- Add a guarantee that reverses the primary risk

**Step 8: Name the offer**
Apply the MAGIC framework. Test 5-10 name options. Pick the most specific and magnetic.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Solving only some of the problems**
One unresolved perceived problem can kill the sale. "I once insisted folks prepare food at home. Rather than solve the 'eating out' problem, I lost sales — until I created an eating out guide."

**Mistake 2: Making the stack feel like filler**
Adding random bonuses dilutes the offer. Every bonus should solve a specific, named objection. Ask: "What's stopping them from buying?" Then add a bonus that removes that obstacle.

**Mistake 3: Naming it generically**
"The Gold Package" tells prospects nothing. Name communicates the avatar and the dream outcome. If a stranger can't understand what they get and who it's for from the name alone, rename it.

**Mistake 4: Fake scarcity or urgency**
If you say "only 5 spots" but take everyone who asks, you destroy credibility. Make your limits real and honor them. Ethical persuasion only.

**Mistake 5: Wrong guarantee for your stage**
An unconditional guarantee requires you to be supremely confident in delivery. If you have a 30% refund rate, it's unsustainable. Match your guarantee to your delivery confidence level.

**Mistake 6: Racing to add more features instead of clarifying value**
More features doesn't mean more perceived value. Value comes from solving perceived problems. If prospects aren't seeing value, the problem is usually poor communication — not insufficient features.

**Mistake 7: Not starting with cash flow first**
Don't over-optimize your delivery model before you have customers. Over-deliver first. Get cash flow. Then systematize.

## Examples

### Example 1: Gym Launch ($500 → $16,000 → $42,000)
- **Before:** Standard marketing help for $500/month (same as competitors at $500)
- **After:** 16-week done-with-you program at $16,000 (3x the highest-priced competitor, 32x the cheapest)
- **Stack:** Ads management, sales scripts, lead follow-up system, hiring support, daily coaching calls, meal plans for members, accountability system
- **Guarantee:** Results or continue working until they get them
- **Outcome:** Average gym added +$239,000/year revenue after 11 months

The offer was $16,000 for something worth (to the gym owner) a fraction of $239,000. That's a steal even at that price.

### Example 2: Photography ($300 → $1,500)
Stack built around eliminating every objection:
- Session prep guide (reduces effort/anxiety)
- Same-day digital preview (reduces time delay)
- "Love it or reshoot" guarantee (reduces risk)
- Family heirloom framing package (increases dream outcome)
- Online gallery share link (increases social proof)
Total: $1,500 offer. Previously charged $300 for just the session.

### Example 3: Business Coaching (from scratch)
Dream outcome: "Your first $10,000 month in 90 days"
Problems identified (sample): Don't know what to offer, can't find clients, can't close sales, don't know how to price, scared to post content, imposter syndrome
Solutions stack:
- "What to Sell" Clarity Framework (DFY template)
- "First 10 Clients" Outreach System (script + sequences)
- "Close More Clients" Sales Script (word-for-word)
- "Price Yourself Right" Calculator
- Private community access (for accountability)
- Weekly group coaching calls (for questions)
- Guarantee: 90 days or we continue free

Guarantee removes the "what if it doesn't work" objection. Stack removes every other objection. Name calls out the avatar and the dream outcome.
