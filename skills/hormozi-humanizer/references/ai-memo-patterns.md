# AI memo patterns

These patterns come from the user's provided screenshots. Use them as a hard
review checklist for business writing, memos, client updates, and public posts.

## Phrase Tells

Cut or rewrite these unless they appear inside a quote:

- `delve into`
- `unpack`
- `this signals`
- `this underscores`
- `navigate the complexities of`
- `ever-changing landscape`
- `synergies`
- `leverage our learnings`
- `holistic approach`
- `moreover`
- `furthermore`
- `that said`
- `additionally`
- `importantly`
- `ultimately`
- `at the end of the day`
- `lean into our strengths`
- `foster a culture of accountability`
- `quietly underscores`

## Structural Tells

- Every paragraph opens with a transition word.
- The draft uses bold labels followed by colon explanations in bullet lists.
- The ending ties everything in a neat little bow without saying a concrete
  decision, risk, or next step.
- A paragraph sounds smart, but the reader cannot summarize what it actually
  said.
- The draft uses forced negation: `not this but that`, `not merely`, `not only`,
  or `instead of X, Y` when a direct sentence would work.
- The draft stacks staccato repetitions: `Not this. Not this. Just that.`
- The draft adds adverbs to make weak verbs sound important.

## Replacement Moves

Use these replacements as patterns, not templates:

| AI-shaped move | Human move |
| --- | --- |
| "This signals a shift in customer behavior." | "Customers are doing X, so we need to change Y." |
| "Let's delve into the results and unpack what they mean." | "Let's look at the results and decide what changes." |
| "Navigate the complexities of scaling." | Name the complexity: hiring, cash, support volume, product quality, or whatever it actually is. |
| "Leverage our learnings." | "Here is what worked, what did not, and what we are changing." |
| "Take a holistic approach." | Name the specific action owners will take. |

## Stronger Source Material

The user's raw bullets are often more valuable than a polished AI memo. If the
draft has buried the thinking, recover the bullets first, then rewrite only as
much as needed for the audience.
