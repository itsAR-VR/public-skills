# 6-Slide Structure

Every TikTok slideshow follows a 6-slide formula. Each slide has a specific job.

## The Formula

| Slide | Purpose | Text Style |
|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **HOOK** — Stop the scroll | Relatable conflict or curiosity gap |
| 2 | **PROBLEM** — Establish the pain | Emotional reaction to the problem |
| 3 | **DISCOVERY** — Introduce the solution | "So I tried..." moment |
| 4 | **RESULT 1** — First wow moment | Genuine surprise reaction |
| 5 | **RESULT 2** — Peak impact | The best transformation |
| 6 | **CTA** — Drive action | App name + call to action |

## Slide Details

### Slide 1: The Hook
- This is everything. If slide 1 doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.
- Use person+conflict format: "My boyfriend said our flat looks like a catalogue"
- Or POV format: "POV: you finally decide to fix your ugly kitchen"
- Or listicle: "3 things I wish I knew before redesigning my room"
- Show something relatable but imperfect

### Slide 2: The Problem
- Amplify the emotional reaction
- "She said you can't change anything... challenge accepted"
- Show the "before" state — messy, boring, frustrating

### Slide 3: The Discovery
- Natural introduction of the app/product
- "So I downloaded this app and took one photo"
- Don't be salesy — position as genuine discovery

### Slide 4: First Result
- Show transformation beginning
- "Wait... is this actually the same kitchen??"
- Genuine surprise reactions work best

### Slide 5: Peak Impact
- The best result — save the most impressive for here
- "Okay I'm literally obsessed with this one"
- This is what makes people save/share the post

### Slide 6: CTA
- Drive the action
- "[App name] showed me what's possible — link in bio"
- Or: "Search [App] on the App Store"
- Keep it natural, not salesy

## Image Rules (All Slides)

- **Portrait 1024x1536** — fills TikTok screen completely
- **Same base scene** — lock architecture, layout, furniture across all slides
- **Consistency anchors** — specify the same window, sofa, table in every prompt
- **"iPhone photo"** in prompt — makes it look real
- **Everyday objects** — mugs, remotes, magazines add lived-in feel
- **No text in images** — text is added via overlay script

## Hook Categories

### Person + Conflict (highest performers)
- "My landlord said I can't change anything"
- "My boyfriend said our flat looks like a catalogue"
- "My mom said this room is depressing"

### POV
- "POV: you finally fix your ugly kitchen"
- "POV: AI redesigns your entire room in 10 seconds"

### Listicle
- "3 room glow-ups that cost me $0"
- "5 things AI thinks your room needs"

### Mistake/Education
- "This is why your room looks cheap"
- "Stop doing this to your living room"

### Before/After
- "Same room, different energy"
- "What my room looked like vs what AI suggested"
