# Hormozi Skill: Advertising

Use when: building ads, improving CTR/CVR, or scaling creative output without random experimentation.

## Core principles
- The hook is leverage: weak hook kills strong offers.
- Hooks work via two parts: **call out** + **value condition/promise**.
- Build ads modularly: **Hook + Meat + CTA**.
- Volume + structure beat one-off “creative genius” (e.g., 50 hooks × 3–5 meats × 1–3 CTAs).
- Model winners first, then iterate with controlled novelty (70-20-10 style allocation).

## Key frameworks
### 1) Hook mechanics
- **Call out**: signals “this is for me.”
- **Hook promise**: why attention is worth paying.
- Use verbal + nonverbal hooks together where possible.

### 2) 70-20-10 Hook Production
- 70%: proven hook patterns from your own winners.
- 20%: permutations adjacent to winners.
- 10%: net-new experiments.

### 3) GOATed Ads Assembly Line
- Step 1: collect best existing ads.
- Step 2: split into **hook/meat/CTA**.
- Step 3: produce many hook variants.
- Step 4: pair with 3–5 meat variants.
- Step 5: test 1–3 CTAs.
- Expected output pattern from source: high ad-volume combinations per cycle.

### 4) Awareness-aligned hooks (source references “most aware” etc.)
- Most aware: offer-driven hooks.
- Product aware: proof-driven hooks.
- Less aware: problem/solution education hooks.

### 5) Advertising Handbook Framework Bank (selected)
- Personal Testimonial / Origin Story
- Emotional Avatar Testimonial
- Show Don’t Tell
- New vs Old
- Value Anchor
- Bribe
- Underdog
- Challenge with Prize

## Practical templates
### Template A: Hook line
`[Call-out avatar], [specific promise] in [timeframe] without [common sacrifice].`

### Template B: Ad Skeleton
- Hook: `[first 1-5 seconds / headline]`
- Meat: `[proof + mechanism + offer details]`
- CTA: `[what/how/when + what happens next]`

### Template C: CTA Quality Check
A good CTA shows/tells:
- what to do,
- how to do it,
- when to do it,
- what they get for doing it.

## Examples
1. Offer-driven hook (most aware):
- “New clients this month: book by Friday for [specific incentive].”

2. Proof-driven hook:
- “How [avatar] achieved [result] in [time] with [mechanism].”

3. Problem-driven hook:
- “If your ads are getting clicks but no calls, this is the missing step.”
