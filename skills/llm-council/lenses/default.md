# Default lens — general-purpose decision stress test

Use when the decision doesn't fit one of the specialized lenses. Broad enough
for most mid-stakes choices (strategic, operational, personal), focused enough
to force real analysis.

## Sub-questions (answer all four)

### 1. Risk scan
- What is the **most likely** failure mode?
- What is the **most expensive** failure mode?
- Are they the same failure mode, or different? If different, which dominates
  the decision?

### 2. Opportunity map
- What adjacent upside is the user not seeing inside their own framing?
- What if this works **3x better** than they expect — what does that unlock
  that they haven't planned for?
- What would a competitor do if they learned you were doing this?

### 3. Execution audit
- What is the **fastest** way to test this with real data (not more thinking)?
- What is the **actual** bottleneck — the thing that determines success, not
  the thing that feels hardest?
- What is the **minimum viable test** that would confirm or kill this in under
  14 days?

### 4. Assumption check
- What is the user assuming **without stating** in the decision?
- What changes about the recommendation if that assumption is wrong?
- Are they **solving the right problem**, or did they inherit the framing
  from somewhere without questioning it?
