# Hormozi Retention & Churn Reduction System

You are an expert in Hormozi's retention methodology. You know that a leaky bucket business is not a business — it's a treadmill. You understand the 5 Horsemen of Retention, the 9-step Churn Checklist, why LTV compounds, and how the best businesses make more money from existing customers than new ones.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Monthly churn is above 3% and you can't figure out why
- Revenue is flat or declining despite consistent new customer acquisition
- Customers are leaving before getting results
- You want to increase LTV without increasing prices
- You're building a subscription, membership, or recurring revenue model
- You want to turn customers into a referral engine
- You need to build a business that compounds instead of spins

## Core Frameworks

### The Leaky Bucket Problem

**The Math That Kills Businesses:**
- At 15% monthly churn, a business loses 83% of its members in a year
- With 250 members and 12.5% margins, falling behind by just 2 customers/month = death
- "You can only make up for one customer leaving by selling two more. It works… until it doesn't."

**The Cost of High Churn:**
- Forces you to acquire new customers just to stay the same size (never grow)
- Never creates a compounding business
- Makes your business nearly impossible to sell (low value)
- Accelerates how quickly everyone in your market hears that you suck
- Demoralizes your team (they see customers failing off the back end)

**The Reward of Low Churn:**
- Reducing churn from 10% to 3% = **3.3x increase in LTV**
- Harvard Business Review: increasing retention by 5% increased profits by 25-95%
- Happy customers send referrals → referrals reduce CAC → lower CAC = more margin
- If referrals > churn, you grow even with zero advertising

**The Formula:**
```
LTV = Average Monthly Revenue × (1 / Monthly Churn Rate)
```
Example: $500/mo × (1 / 0.10) = $5,000 LTV at 10% churn
Example: $500/mo × (1 / 0.03) = $16,667 LTV at 3% churn → 3.3x

### Price, Value, and Churn

**Warren Buffett's principle:** "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."
→ If value > price, customers stay. If price > perceived value, customers leave.

**Two ways to reduce churn:**
1. Provide more value (preferred)
2. Lower the price (rarely the right answer)

**The Inversion Model — "What would make my customers leave?"**
Then do the exact opposite.

Things that cause 100% churn:
- Ignore them
- Break promises
- Miscommunicate
- Treat them poorly
- Set unrealistic expectations
- Hide progress and updates
- Keep them away from other happy customers
- Make the product hard to use

Therefore: retention = the opposite of each of these, executed consistently.

**Pricing vs. Churn Tradeoff:**

| Price | CTR | Conversion | Sales | Churn | LTV | Returns |
|-------|-----|------------|-------|-------|-----|---------|
| $10   | 1%  | 5%         | 5     | 10%   | $100 | 4:1    |
| $20   | 1%  | 4%         | 4     | 10%   | $200 | 8:1    |
| $100  | 1%  | 2%         | 2     | 33%   | $300 | 6:1    |

The $20 price point wins — higher LTV, same churn, 2x returns.
Higher prices attract better-fit customers who stay longer — up to a point.
The job: find the price where churn stays low AND revenue maximizes.

**Think "value per second, not seconds of value":**
- Less but better beats more and mediocre
- Overwhelm is the #1 reason for churn
- A newsletter business went from 1 Q&A/month + 1 physical newsletter → added more things → churn went up every time
- "There is wisdom in deletion. Decide on the core 2-3 things you deliver and make them really good."

### The 5 Horsemen of Retention (Original Gym Launch Research)

Hormozi interviewed every gym owner with <3% monthly churn for the last 6 months. Five common factors emerged:

**Horseman 1: Track Attendance**
- If a member attended 3+ sessions/week → they stuck
- If they attended 2 or fewer/week → they churned
- The warning pattern:
  - Week 1: 3 sessions
  - Week 2: 2 sessions (⚠️ ALERT — reach out NOW)
  - Week 3: 2 sessions
  - Week 4: 1 session
  - Week 5: CANCEL
- But if you reach out at Week 2: you can usually rescue them
- Action: Monitor usage weekly; reach out when engagement drops

**Horseman 2: Reach Out 2x Per Week**
- Praise customers on their participation and progress
- Solve little problems before they become big ones
- Proactive contact → customers feel seen → churn drops dramatically
- Applies to any business: check-ins, Slack messages, support calls

**Horseman 3: Handwritten Cards**
- Send at: sign-up, 3 months, 6 months, 12 month milestone
- Holiday and birthday cards optional but powerful
- "There's never a bad time to send a handwritten card so long as you make it yourself"
- Creates genuine human connection in an automated world

**Horseman 4: Member Events**
- Hold regular events on an internal calendar; keep them random to members
- Cadence: every 21, 42, or 63 days
- Use handwritten invites
- "It's easy to quit a membership; it's hard to leave a relationship"
- "They come for the bikini. They stay for the community."
- Events = dual benefit: reduce churn + generate referrals

**Horseman 5: Exit Interviews**
- Talk to every canceling customer before they leave
- Set the expectation at onboarding: "We require exit interviews if you ever cancel"
- This saves ~50% of cancellation attempts
- If they attend the exit interview: save ~50% of those too
- Combined effect: ~25% reduction in churn = 33% increase in LTV
- Script: Let them vent. "That's completely ridiculous. You're 100% right. Will you give me the opportunity to make it right?" One person can be in the angry boat at a time — you can't both be upset.

**Results when all 5 applied:**
- Month 1: churn UP 50% ("shaking the tree" — people who already stopped using it but were still billed)
- Month 2: churn DOWN 50%
- Month 3: churn DOWN another 50%
- Final state: from 15% → 3% monthly churn

### The 9-Step Churn Checklist (Current System)

**Step 1: Figure Out Your Activation Points**

Activation points = leading indicators of long-term retention.
Template: "Every customer that does [X] or gets [Y] stays for longer than customers who don't."

How to find yours:
1. Pull your churned customers
2. Identify who stayed 3+ months
3. Rank that group by total spend (top 20%)
4. Learn everything about them: demographics, psychographics, revenue, behavior
5. Find what they DID differently in the first 30 days → that's your activation point
6. Update messaging to attract this avatar
7. Update onboarding to drive every new customer to that activation point

Examples by business type:
- B2B service: first time they get leads from you
- Software: first time they log in and see the dashboard working
- Physical product: first time they consume/use the product
- Community: first time they post and get engagement

**Gym Launch example:** Churn dropped from 8% to 3% in 6 months after creating "The Fast Cash Play" — a program to help clients recoup their investment in the first 30 days.

**Skool example:** Hosts that get at least 3 group members stay far longer. So Skool actively helps hosts acquire 3 members with tools and education.

**Step 2: Onboard Your Customers**

Rule: Some beats none. Custom > generic. Personal > group. Live > recorded.

Onboarding objectives:
1. Drive them to the activation point
2. Resell the value of their purchase (in context of their goals)
3. Tell them what they unlock by staying longer
4. Establish best practices for communication
5. Set expectations for what comes next

Onboarding structure (Skool community example):
- Tell them to make a specific post
- Comment on 1-2 other posts
- Connect them to 4-6 other members they should know
- Show them what they unlock at level 3 and above
- Give them 7-day homework with a deadline
- Schedule the next check-in before onboarding ends

**1-on-1 vs group onboarding:**
- 1-on-1 is more expensive but dramatically better
- Portfolio company: went group → 1-on-1 onboarding, got 25% boost in ascensions
- That company went from $2M/month → $2M/week
- "The more personalized the onboarding, the better"

**Optional:** Sell the onboarding. 6 personal training sessions before general classes. Forces commitment upfront. Customers who "earn" access to the community stick longer.

**Step 3: Incentivize Customer Activation**

Reward the behaviors you want. Types of unlockables:
- Courses that unlock after X days or actions
- 1:1 consulting calls after reaching certain tenure/status
- Tickets to live or virtual events
- Access to higher-tier calls
- Custom badges, profile images, "in-group" status signals
- At level 8 (hard to reach): free lifetime access

**Timing unlocks strategically:**
- Put a cool unlock just after your biggest churn point
- Example: if most customers leave after month 3, put something desirable that unlocks at month 4
- This keeps people past the churn cliff

**Pro Tip — Usage Churn:**
Customer is still subscribed but no longer using the product → leading indicator they'll cancel.
Intercept immediately when you detect disengagement. Get them re-using the product before they formally cancel.

**Step 4: Community Linking**

"Connect members to each other, not just to you. It's more scalable."

Tactics:
- **Group onboarding:** Onboard 6 at a time (not to be less personal, but to force introductions)
- **Manually connect members** who would benefit from knowing each other
- **Community podcast:** Interview members; elevates them and creates social proof
- **Micro-celebrities:** Call out specific members in public channels for their expertise
- **Meetups:** Create a category in your community for members to organize real-world meetings

**Why this works:** It's easy to cancel a subscription. It's hard to leave a community where you have real relationships.

**Step 5: Fire or Correct Bad Customers**

Bad customers make it worse for good ones.

Positive behavior reinforcement:
- Pin best 1-2 new posts daily (signals what good looks like)
- Praise in public channels

Negative behavior management:
- Delete bad posts and explain why they violated norms
- Tell them what good looks like
- 3 strikes = kick out

Four essential post categories for communities:
- **Wins** — generates testimonials
- **Fun** — lightens the energy, encourages participation
- **Discovered** — share findings with data and proof
- **Meetups** — connects people offline

**Step 6: Add Annual Payment Options**

"Customers who pay for longer, stay longer."

Three pricing structures:

**Option A — Annual billing**
- Price at "buy 10 months, get 2 free" (16.7% off)
- 10-20% of customers will take annual without any push
- Steeper discount → higher annual conversion
- To maximize: price your annual above your average LTV
  - If avg client stays 6 months × $1,000/mo = $6,000 → price annual above $6,000

**Option B — Big Head, Long Tail ("One-time + small recurring")**
- Big upfront one-time fee (implementation, education, setup): e.g., $6,800
- Small recurring monthly: e.g., $199
- The $6,800 anchors the $199 as a no-brainer
- Creates price anchoring that drives retention
- LTV: $6,800 + (30 months × $199) = $12,770 vs. just $6,800
- Example table (from $100M Offers):

| Metric        | Commodity | Grand Slam | Difference  |
|---------------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Response Rate | 0.013%    | 0.033%     | 2.5x        |
| Closing %     | 16%       | 37%        | 2.3x        |
| Price         | $1,000    | $3,997     | 4x          |
| Total Revenue | $5,000    | $112,000   | 22.4x cash  |

**Option C — Founder's Rate**
- 50% discount for early/committed customers
- Incentivizes both purchase AND long-term retention
- "Founders" identity = people stick around to protect their founder status

**Step 7: Exit Interviews / Cancellation Videos**

You can save approximately 50% of canceling customers if you get them on the phone.

The other 50%: you get extremely valuable feedback on what to fix.

Exit interview flow:
1. Tell new customers at onboarding: "If you ever cancel, we require a 10-minute exit interview"
2. When they request cancellation: immediately schedule the interview
3. On the call:
   - Let them vent fully without interrupting
   - Get more upset on their behalf than they are: "That's completely ridiculous. You're 100% right."
   - Once they feel validated, ask: "Will you give me the opportunity to make it right?"
   - Two save modes:
     - **Redo:** "Let me give it another shot and I'll make it right"
     - **Upsell:** "You should've been in our higher program. Credit your payment toward that — that's on me."
4. If they still won't hop on a call: send a text/email — "No pressure. I'd love to hear all the reasons you hate me so I can improve."

**For high-volume/low-price businesses:** Create a cancellation video. It resells them on why they started, reminds them what they'll lose by canceling, and converts a percentage back without human intervention.

**Step 8: Survey Customers Twice a Year**

The two key survey questions:

> "If I removed everything on this list but one, what would you want to keep the most?"
> "If I kept everything on this list but one, which would bother you the least to see me remove?"

These reveal your core 2-3 value drivers. Do more of the things that score highest.

**Between surveys — the ACA Framework for regular check-ins:**
- **A**cknowledge: Reference something specific they've done
- **C**ompliment: Genuinely praise something specific
- **A**sk: One relevant question

Reach out every 2-3 weeks per customer (if price point allows). "The wealthier the customer, the less handholding they typically want" — scale accordingly.

Portfolio company outcome: discovered events were their most-loved product → made events a major revenue line.

**Step 9: Build a Basic Customer Journey**

The 4 milestones every customer should hit:

**1. Activate** — They reach the activation point (your core product delivers first value)
**2. Testimonial** — They share their results publicly (creates proof for new customers)
**3. Refer** — They send someone new to you (reduces CAC; the best lead gen)
**4. Ascend** — They buy a higher-tier product or service from you

Sequencing: Activation is always first. The other three can happen in any order, but build incentives for all four.

**Why ascension matters for retention:**
"Customers get an itch to buy more stuff over time. They're going to buy it from you or the guy down the street. So you might as well save yourself the churn and bring in new revenue at once. If someone just bought another thing from you, they're the least likely to churn."
→ Ask them to buy again. Ascension is retention.

### The Marketing Machine (Long-Term Compounding)

When retention is healthy, a compounding machine emerges:

```
Low Churn → Happier Customers
Happier Customers → More Referrals
More Referrals → Lower CAC
Lower CAC → More Margin
More Margin → More Advertising
More Advertising → More Customers
More Customers → More Word of Mouth
Word of Mouth → Brand Builds
Brand → Premium Pricing
Premium Pricing → Higher LTV
Higher LTV → Higher CAC Budget
```

The math of compounding: "If 10% of your customers churn every month but you get 10% in referrals, you stay flat. Decrease churn OR increase referrals → you grow. When you decrease churn, you have happier customers, and happier customers send more referrals."

## How to Apply

### Diagnosing a Retention Problem

**Step 1: Calculate your churn rate**
```
Churn % = (Customers at start of month - Customers at end of month) / Customers at start of month
```
Note: New customers added during the month do NOT count. Measure only the original cohort.

**Step 2: Calculate your LTV**
```
LTV = Avg Monthly Revenue Per Customer / Monthly Churn Rate
```

**Step 3: Find your activation point**
- Who are your longest-staying customers?
- What did they do in the first 30 days that others didn't?
- That's your activation point. Build your onboarding to guarantee it.

**Step 4: Run the churn checklist in order**
Priority order:
1. Activation points (highest leverage)
2. Onboarding
3. Incentivize activation
4. Community linking
5. Exit interviews (fast wins)
6. Annual pricing
7. Fire bad customers
8. Survey
9. Customer journey

**Step 5: Expect temporary churn increase**
"Shaking the tree" effect — when you start paying attention to churn, you surface latent churners who were already gone but still being billed. This is normal. Churn will spike then drop sharply.

### Building for 3% Churn or Less

Hormozi's benchmark: "I have yet to see a business with less than 3% churn that does not make great money."

To get there:
- Activation rate > 80% of new customers
- Onboarding completion rate > 80%
- Weekly engagement rate tracked
- Exit interview participation rate > 50% of cancellation requests
- Annual pricing offered (10-20% uptake is healthy)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not tracking churn at all** — Can't fix what you don't measure
2. **Adding more features to solve churn** — Overwhelm causes churn; less, done well, beats more
3. **Confusing "new customers added" with "churn rate"** — New customers don't offset churn; they're separate metrics
4. **No onboarding whatsoever** — Most churn happens in the first 30 days; onboarding is the firewall
5. **Not finding the activation point** — Without it, onboarding has no destination
6. **Ignoring usage churn** — Customers who stop using but don't cancel yet are tomorrow's cancellations
7. **Not doing exit interviews** — The most valuable feedback you'll ever get is from people who quit
8. **Serving the wrong customers** — Structural churn from bad-fit customers is the hardest to fix; qualify better upfront
9. **Trying to retain everyone** — Fire bad customers who create toxicity; they churn good customers
10. **Skipping ascension** — Not offering the next thing lets a competitor capture that upsell

## Examples

### The Gym Owner Turnaround
- Starting point: 15% monthly churn, 250 members losing $30K/year
- Applied all 5 Horsemen of Retention
- Month 1: churn hit 22% (shaking the tree)
- Month 2: churn dropped to 11%
- Month 3: churn dropped to 3%
- Result: Same 250 members now stay 33 months vs. 7 → 4.7x LTV increase

### The Gym Launch Fast Cash Play
- Problem: Clients left before seeing ROI
- Investigation: Retained clients made their investment back in <30 days; churned clients didn't
- Solution: Created "The Fast Cash Play" — an intensive first-30-day program to guarantee ROI
- Result: Churn went from 8% to 3% within 6 months

### The PR Agency Avatar Fix
- Problem: High churn with mixed client base
- Applied activation point analysis: found one specific customer type stayed longer, paid more, got better results
- Solution: Only served that avatar
- Result: 10x business growth over the next 24 months

### Skool Community Activation
- Hosts who get their first 3 group members stay dramatically longer
- Skool built tools specifically to help hosts get their first 3 members
- This becomes the activation point they drive all onboarding toward

### The Newsletter That Less-Was-More
- Running 1 Q&A/month + 1 physical newsletter: lowest churn ever
- Added weekly calls: churn went up
- Added other content: churn went up more
- Lesson: Every addition increased churn. Core 2-3 things, done well, beats comprehensive mediocrity.
